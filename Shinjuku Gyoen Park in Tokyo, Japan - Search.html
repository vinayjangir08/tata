Shinjuku Gyoen Park in Tokyo, Japan
By many estimates, the Greater Tokyo Area is the world's most populous metropolitan area, with nearly 38 million residents. The city proper (population 14 million) is densely packed with people, roadways, and buildings, so a 144-acre park in the heart of this megacity is like an oasis in the desert. Opened in 1906, Shinjuku Gyoen Park offers visitors roughly 20,000 trees to admire, including a thousand cherry trees that bloom in abundance each spring during the hanami viewing season. Three distinct landscape motifs appear in Shinjuku Gyoen—not only is the traditional Japanese garden represented in the park (and in our image), but so are the English landscape and French formal styles.
